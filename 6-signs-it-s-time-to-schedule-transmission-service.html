6 Signs It's Time to Schedule Transmission Service

Aug 21, 2023

For your vehicle to function, various systems must operate in harmony. The engine generates power, and the transmission transfers that power to the wheels, enabling movement. Without a functioning transmission, your car would be immobile. Recognizing common signs of transmission failure is crucial. Consumer Reports notes that for CVT transmissions, service intervals vary from 30,000 to 100,000 miles depending on the manufacturer. If you encounter any transmission issues, it's advisable to contact a transmission service promptly.

1. Delayed Gear Shifting

While uncommon in manual transmissions, it's a common occurrence in a failed automatic transmission, particularly noticeable when shifting from park to reverse or drive, and during driving between gears. Even a slight delay in shifting can indicate a significant transmission issue that warrants prompt attention to prevent further complications.

2. Slipping Transmission

A slipping gearbox can lead to a loss of power or untimely shifts in your car. Although it may not indicate complete failure, a slipping transmission is unsafe and requires immediate inspection.

3. Strange Noises

Unusual noises from your transmission, like grinding sounds, suggest abnormal friction between components. It's challenging to pinpoint the exact noise associated with transmission issues, but any unfamiliar sound merits further investigation. s Buzzing, humming, moaning, clunking, or clinking are potential sounds that may indicate a problem with your transmission.

4. Surges or Stalls

Keep an eye out for unexpected acceleration or stalling when changing gears. Surges and stops might indicate a major transmission issue. Normally, your automobile should start moving as soon as you shift into gear. Surging and stalling are not only signs of a transmission malfunction, but they may also be extremely dangerous while driving.

5. Leaking Fluid

Transmission fluid is typically a bright red liquid that can be observed as a puddle beneath your car. Confirming fluid levels using a dipstick is common practice. Low levels, coupled with fluid on the ground, indicate a leak that requires prompt repair. Transmission fluid is vital for the proper functioning of the transmission, and insufficient levels can lead to the transmission seizing and ultimately failing. Addressing fluid leaks promptly is crucial to prevent severe damage.

6. Engine Light

The check engine light is the initial indicator of potential issues with your vehicle. If it illuminates, it's advisable to take your vehicle to a transmission shop for a diagnostic test. This test allows the computer codes to be read, helping identify faults. While the issue may be a minor fix, it could also signal a significant transmission problem that needs attention. Addressing the check engine light promptly ensures timely diagnosis and resolution.
